Ma Baoyu

Ma Baoyu (October 1920 - September 25, 1941), a native of Wei County, Zhangjiakou City, Hebei Province, lived as a wanderer since his parents died when he was a child. One of the five heroes of Wolf Tooth Mountain. In October 1937, he joined the 6th squad of the 7th Company of the 2nd Battalion of the 1st Regiment of the 1st Division of the Jin-Cha-Ji Division of the Eighth Route Army as a soldier and became a squad leader. In September 1938, he bravely killed the enemy in the battle against the Japanese attack and was praised by his superiors. He joined the Communist Party of China in 1939. He died jumping off a cliff on Langya Mountain on September 25, 1941.

Man Gui

ManGui(1594-1630), a native of Xuanfu (present-day Xuanhua, Hebei), whose ancestral home was in Yi County, Yanzhou Prefecture, Shandong (now Zaozhuang City), was a famous general who resisted Houjin in the late Ming Dynasty. Man Gui practiced riding and shooting since he was a child, and after enlisting, he successively served as the general banner, hundred households, and garrison with military merits. After the forty-seventh year of Wanli (1619), he gradually rose to prominence, successively serving as guerrilla, general and other posts, and in the fourth year of the Tianqi (1624), he was the main commander of the Ming army in the Battle of Ningyuan and the Battle of Ningjin, with outstanding merits.

Qin Kai

Qin Kai, born in the Qin clan of the State of Lu, was a general of the State of Yan during the Warring States period. In his early years, he was a hostage in Donghu, very trusted by Donghu, and familiar with folk customs. After King Ji of Yan Zhao ascended the throne, Qin Kai fled to the Yan state.Later, in 300 BC, Donghu was broken, forcing Donghu to retreat more than a thousand miles north, and also crossed the Liaoshui River to attack the Mi family's Joseon, reaching Manfan Khan (the ancient city of Boling in Bochuan County in the Daning River basin west of the Cheongcheon River in present-day North Korea) and took more than 2,000 li of land. The State of Yan placed five counties in Shanggu, Yuyang, Youbeiping, Liaoxi and Liaodong to build the Great Wall of Yan.

Dong Cunrui

Dong Cunrui (October 15, 1929 - May 25, 1948), a Manchu, was born in Nanshanbao Village, Huailai County, Zhangjiakou City, Chahar Province (now part of Hebei Province), came from a poor peasant family, served as a children's regiment leader, joined the Eighth Route Army in August 1945, and joined the Communist Party of China in March 1947. In the early spring of 1948, he served as the leader of the 6th Company, 2nd Platoon, 2nd Platoon, 2nd Platoon, 2nd Regiment, 32nd Regiment, 32nd Column, Northeast Field Army of the Chinese People's Liberation Army.
